Why Gramodaya Mission ?
Some economists have been warning about disappearing rural incomes and gradually emptying villages.

Case studies in various countries have found that a growing class of emerging rural consumers have the same aspirations as anyone anywhere else in the urban areas and when globally connected have seen their incomes rise. This is what has been advocated by Mahatma Gandhi decades back. Several factors are adding to this emerging middle class. A small correction course is happening in recent times.Some examples include government interventions, remittances from abroad by their KIN. NGOs and social organisation.

Many organisations are offering better tools to farmers to manage farming. Entities such as IDE India, Gates Foundation, Tata Trusts are good examples. These organisations also provide farmers and the rural population with sustainable living by teaching them to use natural resources (PRADAN in India with Tasar silk is an example), or train them in various crafts to produce commercial products (example, Aarong BRAC in Bangladesh). There are also private sector initiatives such as Olam, which works with small-scale farmers to improve supply chain for their crops. All these initiatives help.

Larger opportunities reside in the up-and-coming rural populations of the Nation. But these are also regions with volatile and unstable regimes going through a turbulanent political caste and religion envoronment.

The challenge is how to make people and companies in urban India to take up and partner towards combined task to rebuild each village, to rebuild each village with the same value based, cultural and self sustaining historical models, simultaneously integrate modern technology, health care , hospitality and retail advantage to rural lifes, to enlighten the village populace with how they can influence governance, and bring in self supportning financial, social and political inclusion in rural india.
