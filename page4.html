MAHAN BHARATH ABHIYAN’S ECONOMIC REJUNEVATION STRATEGY.

    Entrepreneurship has become a very urban activity of late. Not many entrepreneurs are coming up from rural areas.
    There is a clearly an awareness that we need to focus on rural innovations. Companies understand this need. FICCI in India is catalysing rural innovations and entrepreneurs. But much more needs to be done. Among the developing countries India has the largest rural population. Among the developed world, the US has the largest rural population.
    The Rurals have common problems such as issues in healthcare, education and essential infrastructure which incude connectivity, roads, power availability, lack of protected water supply, etc.,
    We now identify few trends that are going to shape Rural India in the immediate future.
    Through mobile phones, internet and satellite TV, rural migrant consumers are also very well connected and aware. They are aware of what is available for them and their children. Their aspirations are not any different from urban consumers. Rural migrants are also a big source of information and choices for products and services needed in Rural areas and can act as conduit for connecting Rural and Urban Economies.
    Urban markets are saturated and competitive. Rural markets offer an opportunity for expansion and sustainable growth. Companies are developing an inclusive strategy to include both urban and rural consumers. A catalyst is the need of the hour.

THE RURAL AREAS, EVEN TODAY, FORM THE BACK-BONE OF INDIA.
Mission objects

  • Reverse Migration of 20 crore of rural poopulation with rural roots and living in urban India back to their homes.
  • Build smart rural india with excellent modern infra structure and the ancient cultural grandeur.
  • A self sustaning micro managed rural economy.
  • World facilities at rural door step.
  • People owned techno aggregation service platform.
  • Revival of ancient heritage and regain natural rythm in five elements to restore underground water tables to the pre 1980 level .

As such it has designed a massive and meticulously planned village transformation project under the name and style of Gramodaya to be executed to the mission mode. The Gramodaya mission targets 14.5 crore rural families spread across about 7 lakh villages and hamlets in a scientific manner. For this under the Gramodaya mission 63000 integrated service centres will be setup to provide 80 essential services required by every human being from birth to death under 19 categories and through 30 business verticals estimated to cost about 300 lakh crores in 10 years. 150 lakh crores will be raised through internal generation from 4 th year onwards to 10 th year and 150 lakh crores has to be invested from the 1 st year to 6 th year.

The 19 categories of services are

  • WATER REJUVENATION
  • CROP AND DAIRY RESOURCES
  • LIQUOUR POLICY
  • WASTE MANAGEMENT
  • FINANCIAL INCLUSION
  • ROADS
  • SANITATION
  • VILLAGE COMMUNITY CENTER
  • ORGANIC FARMING
  • AGRO INDUSTRIES
  • COMMUNITY HEALTH CARE
  • COMMUTING
  • KISAN SANGHATAN
  • MODERN RELIGIOUS PLACES
  • GREEN VILLAGE
  • POWER SUPPORT
  • GRAVE YARDS
  • DIGITAL FACILITIES
